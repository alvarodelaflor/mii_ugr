Tantrum Of The Muse

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Tantrum Of The Muse was a heavy metal art-rock band based out of Lancaster County, Pennsylvania.

Beginning in 1998, when founding member Stephen Mark Sarro (guitar, vocals) and long time musicmate, Udrick Wise (drums) decided to turn their studio project into an actual live band. They recruited their hometown friend, Jason Stauffer (bass) to play with them and put together a couple songs and began playing shows.

The band soon went into the studio to record a new batch of songs, Jason Stauffer was replaced by Jim Settle and the batch of songs eventually became the band's debut album. Entitled The Heart Is A 2 Headed Sperm and released via the Takehold Records label in 1999, the album quickly garnered the band a rapidly loyal fan base.

In 2000, the band entered the studio to record their followup album, MODERNMU$ICK(2000)! and after its release that summer, spent three months on the road touring to promote it. Just like the first album, this record's musical chemistry was unique and heartfelt. Stephen's detuned, cryptic style of guitar playing, Jim's melodic fretless bass work and Rick's freakout style of drumming had begun bringing about overwhelming results.

In 2001, the band self-released The Downtrodden & The Sidhe (live + 2). The rest of the year saw the band battling with interpersonal conflicts that eventually led to its demise. Jim moved to Florida, Rick began touring with the Huntingtons and Stephen continued recording on his own.

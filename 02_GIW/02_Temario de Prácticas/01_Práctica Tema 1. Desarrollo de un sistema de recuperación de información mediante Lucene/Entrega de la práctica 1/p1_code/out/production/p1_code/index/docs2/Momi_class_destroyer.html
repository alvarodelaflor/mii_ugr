Momi was a class of destroyers built for the Imperial Japanese Navy.

Under the "8-8 Plan", twenty-one ships were ordered between 1918 and 1920. They were a development of the Enoki second-class destroyers, relying on the same hull, but incorporating improvements from the Minekazes. They were quite small, comparable to Royal Navy corvettes.

The smaller size necessitatd a reduction from four boilers to three and the adoption of lighter-weight Parsons direct-drive turbines, resulting in a drop from 38500hp in Minekazes to 21500 hp in Momis. In addition, bunkerage was lowered to 275 tons oil fuel.

As in Minekaze, the guns were mounted high, with Q gun deleted and only one bank of torpedo tubes fitted aft.

Several were lost or disposed of between the wars. Momi herself was turned over to trials in 1932, Warabi run down by Jintsu 25 August 1927 off Maizuru, and Kaya and Nashi scrapped in 1939.

Beginning in 1939, several were re-rated patrol boats and converted for escort duty, having one boiler removed (dropping their horsepower to 12000 {8950 kW} and speed to just 18knots {33 km/h}), and their torpedo tubes, minesweeping gear, and X mount deleted, replaced by six 25 mm (1") AA guns, 36 depth charges, and depth charge throwers. As well, their names became numbers. During 1941-2, these were rebuilt again, to carry and launch a 14 m (46 ft) landing barge, having the aft stack removed and stern raked down to the waterline, as well as providing accomondation for 150 troops. All of these except Fuji (Patrol Boat #36) were sunk; Fuji was surrendered and scrapped.

As well, in 1940, a number were disarmed and converted as tenders. All surrendered at war's end and were scrapped in 1947.

Of the surviving Momis, which all had the midships 4.7" (120 mm) replaced by two triple 25 mm mounts in 1942-3, Tsuga was sunk by U.S. air attack 15 January 1945, Kuri was surrendered (but mined off Korea 8 October 1945), and Hasu was surrendered and scrapped.

Marguerite of France

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Marguerite of France may refer to:

  • Marguerite of France (1158 – 1197), queen consort of England and Hungary
  • Marguerite de Provence (1221-1295) was queen of France as the wife of Louis IX of France
  • Marguerite of France (1254-71), daughter of Louis IX of France, wife of John I, Duke of Brabant
  • Marguerite of France (1282 – 1318), queen consort of England, wife of King Edward I of England
  • Marguerite of France (1288-1312) was daughter of Philip IV of France - unmarried, childless
  • Marguerite of France (1310-82), countess of Flanders, Artois and Burgundy, wife of Louis I of Flanders and mother of Louis II of Flanders
  • Marguerite of France (1347-1352), daughter of John II of France and Bonne of Bohemia
  • Marguerite de Valois (1407-58), illegitimate daughter of Charles VI of France, married Jean de Belleville et de Montaigu
  • Marguerite of Valois-Angouleme (1492-1549), elder sister of Francis I of France, married secondly Henry II of Navarre
  • Marguerite of France (Valois) (1523-74), daughter of Francis I of France and Claude of France, wife of Emmanuel Philibert, Duke of Savoy
  • Marguerite of Valois (1553-1615), daughter of Henry II of France, wife (1572-99, divorce) of Henry III of Navarre, later Henry IV of France

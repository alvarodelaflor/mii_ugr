Alvíss

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alvíss ("All-Wise") was a dwarf in Norse mythology.

Thor's daughter, Þrúðr, was promised to Alvíss, however, Thor did not want Alviss married to his daughter, so he devised a plan to stop Alvíss from doing so. He told Alvíss that, because of his small height, he had to prove his wisdom. Alvíss agreed. Thor made the tests last until after the sun had risen. Alviss, because he was a dwarf, was petrified because he was exposed to sunlight, and Þrúðr remained unmarried.

He has a small appearance in Neil Gaiman's novel American Gods.
